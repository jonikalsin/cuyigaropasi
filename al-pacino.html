Professional Life

Alfredo dropped out of school at the age of 17 after failing practically all of his classes except English. In order to fund his acting lessons, he worked as a messenger, busboy, janitor, and postal clerk. During this period, he also appeared in basement plays in New York’s theatrical underworld but was turned down for the Actors Studio. He later became a member of the ‘Herbert Bergh of Studio’ (HB Studio).

Pacino began appearing in theatre shows shortly after, and in 1969 he got a Tony Award for his performance in the Broadway play ‘Does a Tiger Wear a Necktie?’ In the 1971 film ‘The Panic in Needle Park,’ he played ‘Bobby.’ Although the film was not a commercial success, his acting abilities drew recognition. With the publication of ‘The Godfather’ in 1972, Alfredo achieved incredible celebrity. The film gained widespread critical acclaim and was nominated for three Academy Awards.
