Career

Theodros began her journalism career in Western Maryland as a reporter and fill-in anchor at WHAG-TV, currently known as WDVM. During her two years in Frederick, Maryland, Abby covered several memorable events including picking an exclusive interview with a woman who would ultimately be sentenced for her role in the killing of her toddler. The State’s Attorney’s office used Abby’s report in the closing statements of the case.

However, in 2015, Abby made her way back west and spent four years in Fabulous Las Vegas. She woke up her viewers every weekend on the morning show and spent weekdays reporting on stories that mattered to the community.

During her time out there, she covered a range of stories including the Raider’s relocation from Oakland, California to Las Vegas, O.J. Simpson’s release and following parole, and the Las Vegas mass shooting. She will never forget the lives lost on October 1, 2017, and continues to keep in touch with some of the families impacted by that tragedy.

Abby also got her undergraduate degree in communication from the University of California, Santa Barbara. She got her master’s in journalism from Northwestern’s Medill School of Journalism.
