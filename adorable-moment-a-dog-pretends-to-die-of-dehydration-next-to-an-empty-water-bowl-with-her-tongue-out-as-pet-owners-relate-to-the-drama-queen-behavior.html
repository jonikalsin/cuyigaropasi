May 23, 2024

A Pyrenean Mountain Dog has gone viral after faking her ill health on a TikTok video that’s reached more than 14 million views. 

Rhiannon, a Canadian TikToker under the handle @solargurl, posted a video on December 7 of her dog that pretended she ‘died of dehydration’ in an empty water bowl.

The dog rested her head in the empty water bowl for nearly a minute, moving her tongue and seemingly looking sad and tired on camera.

‘Why? Can’t she just bark at the sink like her brother?’ Rhiannon wrote in the caption.

Rhiannon’s Pyrenean Mountain Dog eventually began resting her entire open mouth on the edge of the bowl, staring at the camera and looking more energized than she had appeared during the first 10 seconds of the clip.

According to The Kennel Club, a Pyrenean Mountain or Great Pyrenees dog is a ‘smart, patient, calm’ breed with a 10-12 life expectancy.

It is affectionate with families and can weigh more than 100 pounds when it becomes a grown adult. 

Despite Rhiannon’s dog pretending to be dehydrated, owners have noted that the dogs actually eat a small amount of food compared to other big dogs.

Purina told Newsweek that Great Pyrenees who eat wet food do not need much water, and when they are looking for extra or no H2O, owners are advised by professionals to take the dogs to the vet.
