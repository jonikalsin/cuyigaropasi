Annette O’Toole is an American actor, dancer, singer, songwriter, voice actor, and writer. She is known for her roles like Lisa Bridges in the CBS police procedural series Nash Bridges, Dorothy ‘Dottie’ Thorson in the crime drama show The Huntress, Lana Lang in the superhero film Superman III, Eliza Schultz in the Netflix series The Punisher, Beverly Marsh in the psychological horror drama miniseries It, Hope McCrea in the romantic drama series Virgin River, Martha Kent/The Red Quee in the superhero series Smallville, Rose Fitzgerald Kennedy in the ABC miniseries The Kennedys of Massachusetts, Kathy in the romantic comedy film Cross My Heart, Gail Maguire in the black comedy thriller film Blow the Man Down, and Susan Emerson in the period drama series Halt and Catch Fire.

    Annette O’Toole Facts

  • She was 3 years old when she started learning dance.
  • When she was 13, her family shifted to Los Angeles and, hence, began Annette O’Toole’s acting pursuits.
  • She made her first theatrical film appearance as a passerby in the 1970 Western film Little Big Man. However, her role went uncredited.
  • For her portrayal of Rose Fitzgerald Kennedy in The Kennedys of Massachusetts, Annette was nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award for “Outstanding Lead Actress in a Limited or Anthology Series or Movie”.
  • In 2012, she was seen playing the character named Elaine Sherman in an episode titled The Boy with the Bucket of the procedural drama series The Finder.
  • Annette O’Toole was inducted into the Texas Film Hall of Fame in Austin, Texas on March 7, 2013.
  • In March 2013, she appeared as Madeleine Skurski in an episode titled Idle Hands of the medical drama series Grey’s Anatomy.
